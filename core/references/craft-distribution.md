## Mechanism-first
Pick ONE dominant mechanism per piece (curiosity-gap / aspiration / relief / social
proof / identity / belonging) and label which job each sentence does. Place proof at
the resistance point, not just the end. Close with a low-friction, autonomy-preserving
CTA.

## Information-gain / comp-differentiation
Every blurb must answer: "what does this promise that the 10 nearest comps don't?"
Lead with the unique angle.

## No-fabrication & ethics
Never invent blurb quotes, awards, bestseller status, or sales figures. Mark missing
proof `[PLACEHOLDER: needs blurb from author or peer]`. Persuasion becomes manipulation
when it bypasses informed choice â€” no fake scarcity or invented urgency.
